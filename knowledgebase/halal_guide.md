# Halal Nutrition Guide

## What is Halal?
Halal (Arabic: permissible) defines what is lawful to consume according to Islamic dietary law.

## Halal Foods (Permitted)
- All fruits and vegetables
- Grains, legumes, nuts, seeds
- Seafood (fish, shrimp, etc.) — generally halal
- Dairy products (if no haram additives)
- Eggs
- Meat and poultry **slaughtered according to Islamic rites** (zabiha)
  - Must be slaughtered by a Muslim
  - Animal must be alive and healthy at time of slaughter
  - Name of Allah must be invoked (Bismillah)
  - Blood must be fully drained

## Haram Foods (Forbidden)
- **Pork and pork by-products**: bacon, ham, lard, gelatin from pork, pepsin
- **Alcohol**: no ethanol in any amount — check sauces, marinades, vanilla extract
- **Blood and blood products**: black pudding, blood sausage
- **Carnivorous animals**: lions, wolves, eagles
- **Animals not slaughtered properly**: even beef/chicken if not zabiha
- **Stunning that causes death before slaughter**: debated among scholars

## Hidden Non-Halal Ingredients to Watch
- Gelatin: often from pork — check in yogurt, marshmallows, capsule supplements, jelly
- Emulsifiers: E471 (can be animal-derived), E472 (check source)
- Rennet in cheese: can be animal-derived (use vegetarian/microbial rennet cheese)
- L-cysteine (E920): sometimes from pig/human hair — used in bread
- Carmine (E120): red dye from beetles — found in some yogurts, juices
- Alcohol in flavourings: vanilla extract, some vinegars

## Halal Certification
- Look for certified halal logos from trusted bodies (e.g., HMC, HFA in UK; ISWA, IFANCA in USA)
- Halal-certified products guarantee the full supply chain has been verified

## Nutritional Considerations for Halal Eaters
- Full access to all food groups — no nutritional restrictions by design
- High-quality protein from halal-slaughtered beef, lamb, chicken, fish
- Important to diversify: include legumes, dairy, eggs alongside halal meat
- When eating out: ask about cooking oils (lard vs vegetable oil), shared fryers with pork products, and wine/beer in sauces

## Halal-Friendly High-Protein Meal Plan
- Breakfast: Eggs (scrambled/boiled), whole wheat toast, orange juice
- Lunch: Grilled halal chicken breast, brown rice, roasted vegetables
- Snack: Dates, almonds, halal-certified yogurt
- Dinner: Lamb kofta with quinoa salad and tahini dressing
