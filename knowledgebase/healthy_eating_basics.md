# Healthy Eating Basics

## Macronutrients

### Protein
- Essential for muscle repair, immune function, and hormone production
- Sources: meat, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, tofu, tempeh, seitan
- General guideline: 0.8–2.0g per kg of body weight depending on activity level

### Carbohydrates
- Primary energy source for the brain and muscles
- Prefer complex carbs: oats, brown rice, quinoa, sweet potato, whole wheat bread
- Limit simple/refined carbs: white bread, pastries, sugary drinks

### Fats
- Essential for brain health, hormone production, and fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Healthy fats: avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds, fatty fish
- Limit saturated fats; avoid trans fats entirely

## Micronutrients

### Key Vitamins
- Vitamin D: Sun exposure, fatty fish, fortified dairy/plant milks
- Vitamin B12: Meat, eggs, dairy — vegans must supplement
- Vitamin C: Citrus fruits, bell peppers, broccoli, kiwi
- Iron: Red meat, spinach, lentils — pair with Vitamin C for better absorption

### Key Minerals
- Calcium: Dairy, fortified plant milks, leafy greens, tofu
- Magnesium: Nuts, seeds, whole grains, dark chocolate
- Potassium: Bananas, potatoes, avocado, beans

## Food Groups & Portions (MyPlate)
- Half your plate: vegetables and fruits
- Quarter plate: whole grains
- Quarter plate: lean protein
- Side: dairy or dairy alternative

## Hydration
- Recommended: 2–3 litres of water daily (more for athletes)
- Include water-rich foods: cucumber, watermelon, lettuce
- Limit sugary drinks, excessive caffeine, and alcohol
