# Food Allergies & Intolerances Guide

## The 14 Major Allergens (EU Regulation)
1. **Gluten** (wheat, rye, barley, oats)
2. **Crustaceans** (shrimp, crab, lobster)
3. **Eggs**
4. **Fish**
5. **Peanuts**
6. **Soybeans**
7. **Milk** (lactose + casein)
8. **Nuts** (almonds, hazelnuts, walnuts, cashews, pecans, pistachios, macadamia)
9. **Celery**
10. **Mustard**
11. **Sesame seeds**
12. **Sulphur dioxide / Sulphites** (dried fruits, wine, vinegar)
13. **Lupin** (lupin flour in some baked goods)
14. **Molluscs** (clams, mussels, oysters, squid)

## Common Allergies In Depth

### Gluten / Coeliac Disease
- **Coeliac disease**: autoimmune — even trace gluten causes intestinal damage
- **Gluten sensitivity (NCGS)**: symptoms without autoimmune damage
- Avoid: wheat, rye, barley, regular oats (cross-contamination risk), spelt, kamut
- Safe grains: rice, quinoa, millet, buckwheat, certified gluten-free oats, corn/maize, amaranth, teff, sorghum
- Watch for hidden gluten: soy sauce, malt vinegar, beer, many sauces and dressings, communion wafers, some medications
- Nutritional risk: fibre, B vitamins, iron — supplement or eat fortified GF foods

### Dairy / Lactose Intolerance vs Milk Allergy
- **Lactose intolerance**: cannot digest lactose (milk sugar) — bloating, gas, diarrhoea
  - Can often tolerate small amounts, hard cheeses, yogurt (bacteria digest lactose)
- **Milk allergy**: immune response to casein/whey proteins — can be severe/anaphylactic
  - Must avoid all dairy strictly
- Dairy-free alternatives: almond milk, oat milk, soy milk, coconut milk, rice milk
- Calcium sources without dairy: fortified plant milks, tofu, broccoli, kale, tahini

### Nut Allergies
- Peanut allergy and tree nut allergy are separate conditions
- Cross-contamination is a serious risk — "may contain nuts" warnings
- Many people allergic to peanuts can eat tree nuts and vice versa — confirm with doctor
- Seed alternatives for nutrition: sunflower seeds, pumpkin seeds, hemp seeds (if tolerated)

### Egg Allergy
- More common in children; many outgrow it
- Egg-free baking substitutes: flax egg (1 tbsp ground flax + 3 tbsp water), chia egg, aquafaba, applesauce, banana
- Watch for hidden eggs: pasta, baked goods, mayonnaise, some vaccines (flu shot)

### Soy Allergy
- Soy is in many processed foods: soy lecithin, vegetable protein, miso, edamame, tempeh, tofu
- Soy lecithin is often tolerated by soy-allergic individuals (highly refined)
- Protein alternatives: lentils, chickpeas, peas, quinoa, hemp seeds

## Food Intolerance vs Food Allergy
| | Allergy | Intolerance |
|---|---|---|
| Immune response | Yes (IgE-mediated) | No |
| Onset | Minutes to 2 hours | Hours to days |
| Small amounts | Can trigger reaction | Usually tolerated |
| Severity | Can be life-threatening | Uncomfortable but not dangerous |
| Examples | Peanut, shellfish, milk | Lactose, fructose, histamine |

## Cross-Contamination Rules
- Separate cutting boards and utensils for allergen-free cooking
- Shared fryers (chips cooked with fish) — major risk
- Check manufacturing labels: "made in a facility that also processes peanuts"
- When eating out: always inform staff — ask about ingredients and cooking methods

## Allergy-Friendly Meal Ideas

### Gluten-Free High-Protein Meal
- Breakfast: Rice porridge with banana, almond butter, and chia seeds
- Lunch: Grilled chicken with quinoa and roasted vegetables
- Dinner: Salmon with sweet potato and steamed broccoli

### Nut-Free Vegan Meal
- Breakfast: Soy yogurt with sunflower seeds and berries
- Lunch: Lentil and vegetable soup with gluten-free bread
- Dinner: Chickpea curry with basmati rice

### Multiple Allergen-Free (No gluten, dairy, eggs, nuts, soy)
- Breakfast: Oat-free granola with coconut milk and fresh fruit
- Lunch: Rice noodle bowl with halal chicken, cucumber, avocado
- Dinner: Lamb stew with root vegetables and rice
