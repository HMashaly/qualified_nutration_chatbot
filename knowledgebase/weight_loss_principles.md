# Weight Loss & Body Composition Principles

## Caloric Deficit
- Weight loss requires a caloric deficit: burning more calories than you consume
- Safe deficit: 300–500 kcal/day → approximately 0.3–0.5 kg loss per week
- Avoid extreme deficits (<1200 kcal women / <1500 kcal men) — leads to muscle loss and nutrient deficiencies

## TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)
- BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): calories your body needs at complete rest
- TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier
  - Sedentary (desk job, no exercise): × 1.2
  - Light activity (1–3 days/week): × 1.375
  - Moderate (3–5 days/week): × 1.55
  - Very active (6–7 days/week): × 1.725
  - Athlete (2x/day training): × 1.9

## Mifflin-St Jeor Formula (BMR)
- Men: (10 × weight_kg) + (6.25 × height_cm) − (5 × age) + 5
- Women: (10 × weight_kg) + (6.25 × height_cm) − (5 × age) − 161

## Fat Loss vs Muscle Preservation
- High protein intake (1.6–2.2g/kg) preserves muscle during deficit
- Resistance training is the most effective way to preserve muscle while losing fat
- Cardio burns calories but does not build muscle
- Aim for slow, steady weight loss — rapid loss leads to muscle loss and metabolism slowdown

## Realistic Expectations
- Sustainable fat loss: 0.5–1% of body weight per week
- Plateau is normal: metabolism adapts — take diet breaks, reassess intake
- Focus on body composition (fat % vs muscle %), not just scale weight

## Common Mistakes
- Eating "healthy" but in large portions — calories still count
- Skipping meals → leads to overeating later
- Relying only on cardio without strength training
- Not sleeping enough — poor sleep increases hunger hormones (ghrelin)
