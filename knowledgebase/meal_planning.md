# Meal Planning & Practical Nutrition Guide

## Why Meal Plan?
- Reduces impulsive, unhealthy food choices
- Saves time and money
- Helps hit nutritional targets consistently
- Reduces food waste

## General Meal Planning Principles
- Plan 3 main meals + 1–2 snacks per day
- Prep proteins and grains in bulk (batch cooking)
- Keep healthy snacks accessible: fruit, nuts, yogurt, veggies + hummus
- Build meals around a protein source first, then add carbs and vegetables

## Portion Size Guide (No Scale Needed)
- Protein: palm of your hand
- Carbohydrates: cupped hand
- Vegetables: two fists
- Fats (oils, nuts, avocado): thumb-sized

## Macros by Goal

### Weight Loss
- Protein: 30–35% of calories (high protein preserves muscle, increases satiety)
- Carbs: 30–35% (lower carb, prefer complex sources)
- Fat: 30–35% (healthy fats, not too low)
- Example for 1500 kcal: ~130g protein / ~130g carbs / ~55g fat

### Maintenance / General Health
- Protein: 20–25%
- Carbs: 45–55%
- Fat: 25–35%
- Example for 2000 kcal: ~100g protein / ~225g carbs / ~65g fat

### Muscle Gain
- Protein: 25–30% (1.6–2.2g per kg body weight)
- Carbs: 45–50% (fuel for training)
- Fat: 20–25%
- Caloric surplus: +200–300 kcal above TDEE

## Sample Weekly Meal Prep (Batch Cook Sunday)
- Cook 500g chicken breast or chickpeas
- Cook a large pot of brown rice or quinoa
- Roast a tray of mixed vegetables (broccoli, courgette, peppers)
- Prepare a legume soup/stew (lentil or bean)
- Wash and chop salad vegetables

## Eating Out Tips
- Ask for sauces/dressings on the side
- Choose grilled over fried
- Ask for extra vegetables instead of chips
- Look for keywords: steamed, baked, grilled, poached = better choices
- Keywords to limit: crispy, battered, creamy, smothered

## Snack Ideas by Diet Type
### Omnivore
- Greek yogurt + honey + walnuts
- Boiled eggs + carrot sticks
- Tuna on rice cakes

### Vegetarian
- Cottage cheese + fruit
- Cheese + whole grain crackers
- Smoothie with whey protein

### Vegan
- Hummus + vegetable sticks
- Apple + almond butter
- Roasted chickpeas
- Edamame with sea salt

### Gluten-Free
- Rice cakes + avocado
- Corn tortilla chips + guacamole
- Mixed nuts + dried fruit

### Halal
- Dates + almonds
- Halal-certified jerky
- Yogurt + honey

## Reading Food Labels
- Check serving size first — all values relate to this
- Ingredients list: ingredients in descending order by weight
- Red flags in ingredients: high-fructose corn syrup, hydrogenated oils, artificial colours
- For allergens: bold text in ingredients list (EU labelling law)
- "Low fat" often means high sugar — always check total sugar content
