# Vegan & Vegetarian Nutrition Guide

## Definitions
- **Vegetarian**: No meat or fish. May include dairy and eggs.
  - Lacto-vegetarian: includes dairy, no eggs
  - Ovo-vegetarian: includes eggs, no dairy
  - Lacto-ovo vegetarian: includes both dairy and eggs
- **Vegan**: No animal products whatsoever — no meat, fish, dairy, eggs, honey, or gelatin

## Key Nutrients to Watch

### Protein (Vegan/Vegetarian)
- Complete protein sources (all essential amino acids):
  - Quinoa, soy (tofu, tempeh, edamame), buckwheat
- Combine incomplete sources for complete amino acid profile:
  - Rice + beans, lentils + whole wheat bread, hummus + pita
- Daily target: 0.8–1.6g per kg body weight
- Best vegan protein sources: lentils, chickpeas, black beans, tofu, tempeh, seitan, edamame, hemp seeds

### Vitamin B12 (Critical for Vegans)
- **Vegans MUST supplement B12** — it is not found in plant foods
- Also found in: nutritional yeast (if fortified), fortified plant milks, fortified cereals
- Deficiency causes: nerve damage, fatigue, anemia

### Iron (Plant-Based)
- Non-heme iron (plants) is less absorbed than heme iron (meat)
- Best sources: lentils, spinach, tofu, pumpkin seeds, fortified cereals, dried apricots
- **Always pair with Vitamin C** to enhance absorption (e.g., lemon juice on lentils)
- Avoid coffee/tea within 1 hour of iron-rich meals — inhibits absorption

### Calcium (Vegan)
- Dairy-free sources: fortified plant milks (almond, oat, soy), tofu (calcium-set), broccoli, kale, bok choy, tahini, almonds
- Target: 1000mg/day

### Omega-3 Fatty Acids
- Plant-based ALA (alpha-linolenic acid): flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, hemp seeds
- Body converts ALA to EPA/DHA inefficiently — consider algae-based omega-3 supplement
- Especially important for vegans (fish is main source of EPA/DHA)

### Zinc
- Sources: pumpkin seeds, hemp seeds, cashews, chickpeas, lentils, oats
- Phytates in grains can inhibit absorption — soaking/sprouting improves bioavailability

### Vitamin D
- Difficult to get from food for both vegans and non-vegans
- Sources: sunlight, fortified plant milks, mushrooms (UV-exposed)
- **Supplementation recommended** for most people, especially in low-sun regions

### Iodine (Vegan)
- Not reliably found in plant foods unless soil is rich
- Sources: seaweed (inconsistent), iodized salt, fortified foods
- Consider supplementation

## Sample High-Protein Vegan Day
- Breakfast: Oat porridge with hemp seeds, chia seeds, fortified almond milk
- Lunch: Lentil soup with whole wheat bread + bell pepper salad
- Snack: Edamame + handful of pumpkin seeds
- Dinner: Tofu stir-fry with brown rice, broccoli, and tamari sauce
- Total protein: ~80–100g

## Sample Vegetarian Day
- Breakfast: Greek yogurt with mixed berries and almonds
- Lunch: Egg and cheese wrap with spinach and tomato
- Snack: Cottage cheese with fruit
- Dinner: Lentil dal with basmati rice and roasted vegetables
